Submarine MEDEVAC Response Procedure

  1. You receive a call from the TYCOM Submarine Watch Officer (SWO).
    • They will notify you of a MEDEVAC from some submarine.
    • They should send the MEDEVAC message to your email for you to respond.
    • Make sure they have the right email address - they can send to your personal address because there should be no classified info in a MEDEVAC message.
  2. Review the message.
    • Read it thoroughly. Make sure it makes clinical sense. Make note of any missing info you would like to see.
    • Call the SWO back if you need specific operational info, like a rough estimate of time to the next MEDEVAC opportunity.
  3. Call any specialists, if needed.
    • Refer to your local MTF duty number sheet to speak to any specialists.
    • Treat it like a regular hospital consult - formally present the patient to them with a specific clinical question and provide as much info about submarine capabilities and schedules as you can.
  4. Draft the message. You can include anything you want in your response that you think would be helpful. Use the following template:

    Copyable Message Template:

    • Routine: non-urgent; patient may be disembarked at next scheduled event.
    • ASAP: urgent, non-emergent; all efforts should be made to disembark patient within 48 hours.
    • Immediate: emergent; all efforts should be made to disembark patient within 6 hours. May involve HELOVAC.
  5. Send the message and notify relevant personnel.
    • Always ask for confirmation of receipt from the TYCOM SWO so you know they received it.
    • Best practice is to notify:
      • the SRS medical ISIC or UMO for wherever the submarine is based;
      • the off-crew IDC (for boomers only) since they will be tracking the patient on return to port;
      • your local ISIC office (if action/awareness is required from them)
      • your chain of command (if action/awareness is required from them)
